\section{Research question and sub-questions}
\label{sec:intro-rq}

\noindent\textbf{Main research question.} \\
\textit{The European Union’s economic landscape is increasingly defined by a \textbf{"productivity gap"} rooted in a structural failure to scale deep-tech innovations. While the US and China have created dynamic ecosystems that facilitate high firm turnover and the rise of new industrial leaders, the EU’s corporate landscape remains dominated by established incumbents. \\
In response, the Union has moved beyond traditional market-failure corrections, launching a series of high-stakes initiatives designed to reform its industrial policy. These include the \textbf{European Innovation Council (EIC)}, the \textbf{STEP Scale-Up} instrument, and the \textbf{European Chips Act}. These are not mere funding programs; they are strategic attempts to foster both incremental growth in both new and mature sectors and radical breakthroughs in frontier technologies. However, this "reformist turn" faces a fundamental paradox: the EU is attempting to catalyse radical, high-uncertainty growth on a mandate for \textbf{market stability, regulatory neutrality, and regional redistribution}.}

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Sub-questions.}
\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{SQ\arabic*.},leftmargin=*]
    \item How do the innovation governance systems of the United States, China, and the European Union differ in their treatment of fundamental uncertainty versus calculable risk in the financing and regulation of deep technology development?
    \item How is the concept of “mission-oriented” innovation translated into EU administrative instruments, and to what degree does this translation emphasize regulatory and financial de-risking over technological directionality and scale?
\end{enumerate}

\section{Structure of the thesis}
\label{sec:intro-structure}
The remainder of the thesis is organised as follows.
Chapter~\ref{ch:background} reviews the literature on institutional theory
and on EU innovation funding, situating Pathfinder and STEP within both
streams.
Chapter~\ref{ch:methodology} describes the research design, case selection,
data sources, coding strategy and quality criteria.
Chapter~\ref{ch:results} presents the empirical findings for each instrument
in turn and then comparatively.
Chapter~\ref{ch:discussion} interprets the findings against the theoretical
framework and engages with rival explanations.
Chapter~\ref{ch:conclusion} summarises the contributions, acknowledges
limitations, and proposes avenues for future research.
