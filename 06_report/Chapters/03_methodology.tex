\section{Use of generative AI in the research process}
\label{sec:meth-genai}

The thesis was produced with the systematic support of a generative-AI
(GenAI) assistant.\footnote{Anthropic's \emph{Claude} family of large
language models, accessed via the \emph{Claude Code} command-line interface.
\todoinline{Insert the specific model versions used (e.g.\ Claude Sonnet 4.5
and Opus 4.6) and the period of use.}} Because the field is moving quickly
and the methodological literature is unsettled, this section states the
position taken, describes exactly how the tool was used, and explains how
the analytical work was kept anchored to the researcher's interpretive
judgement.

\subsection{Position taken in an unsettled debate}
\label{sec:meth-genai-position}

The qualitative-methods literature on GenAI ranges from cautious
enthusiasm to principled refusal. \textcite{zhang2024redefining} and
similar contributions in the human--computer-interaction tradition show
that a well-designed prompt framework can extend the productivity of
thematic analysis and surface themes that human coders initially
overlooked. \textcite{chatzichristos2025qualitative} warns that the same
move risks a tacit return to positivism: by treating coding as a
repeatable, data-driven operation, the researcher cedes the reflexive,
interpretive work that defines the constructivist tradition.
\textcite{nguyen2025engaged}, writing from a responsible-research
perspective, go further and argue against any interposition of a GenAI
model between the researcher and the empirical material, on the grounds
that meaning is constituted in the slow, embodied work of reading and
re-reading.

I take an intermediate position. GenAI is treated here as an
\emph{instrument of the researcher} --- comparable in spirit to qualitative
data-analysis software (e.g.\ MAXQDA, NVivo) but with a different operating
logic --- and not as a co-analyst. The interpretive moves that anchor the
study (the institutional-theory readings of Pathfinder and STEP, the
identification of patterns such as logic succession and
tension-dissolution, and the framing of TRL as institutional disguise)
were made by the researcher. The tool was used to accelerate the
mechanical surroundings of those moves: locating passages, maintaining a
consistent codebook, re-formatting notes into a uniform entry schema,
drafting candidate prose for review, and surfacing comparisons across
entries. Where Nguyen and Welch's warning applies most directly --- the
delegation of pattern identification to the model --- the workflow was
deliberately built so that the model proposes and the researcher
disposes.

\subsection{Tools and tasks}
\label{sec:meth-genai-tasks}

The assistant was used for five categories of task across the project,
each of which is documented in the audit trail
(\cref{sec:meth-genai-audit}):

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
  \item \textbf{Codebook construction.} The deductive codebooks in
        \texttt{04\_codebook/output/} (Scott's three pillars + DiMaggio
        \& Powell's isomorphism types; the four institutional logics
        following Thornton et al.) were drafted by the assistant from
        researcher-supplied definitions, then revised by the researcher
        against the primary literature before being applied.
  \item \textbf{Corpus preparation.} The 188-page EIC Work Programme
        2026 PDF was converted to a plain-text working copy
        (\texttt{03\_corpus/output/WP2026\_extracted.txt}) to support
        verbatim quotation and search. No interpretive transformation
        was performed at this step.
  \item \textbf{Deductive readthrough (Pass 1).} For each numbered entry
        in \texttt{Readthrough\_Notes\_v1.md}, the assistant produced
        a candidate coding against the institutional-theory framework,
        flagged ambiguous passages with \texttt{[?]} markers, and
        recorded emerging patterns. Every entry was reviewed, edited
        or rejected by the researcher before it was retained.
  \item \textbf{Synthesis across entries.} Once Pass 1 covered a
        sufficient stretch of the corpus, the assistant was asked to
        identify recurring patterns across entries
        (\texttt{05\_analysis/output/synthesis.md}). These were treated
        as candidate observations, to be confirmed, refined or
        discarded against the underlying entries.
  \item \textbf{Drafting and editing.} Chapter prose, LaTeX scaffolding
        and bibliography management were drafted with assistance and
        then revised by the researcher. No claim, citation or finding
        appears in the final text without independent verification
        by the researcher.
\end{enumerate}

The assistant was explicitly \emph{not} used to: generate or simulate
empirical material; produce coded entries that were retained without
human review; or formulate the central theoretical argument of the
thesis.

\subsection{Division of cognitive labour}
\label{sec:meth-genai-labour}

The workflow was structured around a deliberate division of labour
between researcher and tool. \cref{tab:meth-ai-division} summarises the
allocation.

\begin{table}[ht]
  \centering
  \caption{Division of cognitive labour between researcher and GenAI
  assistant.}
  \label{tab:meth-ai-division}
  \begin{tabular}{p{0.45\linewidth} p{0.45\linewidth}}
    \toprule
    \textbf{Researcher} & \textbf{GenAI assistant} \\
    \midrule
    Research question and theoretical framing & Locating relevant passages in the corpus \\
    Selection of theoretical sources           & Drafting candidate codings against the codebook \\
    Final coding decisions                     & Maintaining schema and formatting consistency \\

This division reflects the position stated above: the model accelerates
work that is structured, repetitive or generative-of-candidates; the
researcher retains every act of judgement on which the validity of the
analysis depends.

\subsection{Verification, audit trail and reproducibility}
\label{sec:meth-genai-audit}

Three practices were used to keep the AI-assisted workflow auditable.

First, all artefacts produced with assistance are version-controlled in
a Git repository covering the full project tree, with an append-only
audit log (\texttt{RESTRUCTURE\_LOG.md}) that records substantive
reorganisations. The numbered readthrough entries
(\texttt{Readthrough\_Notes\_v1.md}) form a chronological record of
every coded passage; their stable numbering means any finding in
\cref{ch:results} can be traced back to the entry from which it
derives, and from there to the verbatim passage in the primary source.

Second, candidate codings produced by the assistant were treated as
proposals and reviewed against the codebook. Where the model's
suggestion was ambiguous or did not fit cleanly, the entry was flagged
with a \texttt{[?]} marker and resolved during the inductive Pass 2.
The set of resolved flags is itself documented and is included as an
appendix excerpt (\cref{app:audit}).

Third, no quotation, citation or factual claim in this thesis appears
without direct verification by the researcher against the primary
source. The full text of the EIC Work Programme 2026 is held locally
and was the canonical reference at every step; secondary literature was
read in the original rather than via summarisation by the assistant.
This addresses the well-documented risk of fabricated citations and
factual drift in large-language-model outputs
\parencite{nguyen2025engaged}.

\subsection{Limitations and risks of AI assistance}
\label{sec:meth-genai-limits}

Three limitations are acknowledged. (i)~\emph{Latent positivist drift.}
\textcite{chatzichristos2025qualitative} argues that AI-assisted coding
can quietly re-import the assumption that meaning resides in the text
and is recoverable by repeatable procedure. This is mitigated, but not
eliminated, by the design of the workflow: the researcher retains all
interpretive moves, and the institutional-logics framework is itself
explicitly constructivist about the social fabrication of categories.
(ii)~\emph{Anchoring on first-pass codings.} Even where the researcher
revises a model-generated coding, the initial suggestion may bias the
direction of attention. The inductive Pass 2 was introduced partly to
counter this anchoring by re-reading entries against unresolved
questions rather than against the original coding. (iii)~\emph{Opacity
of the model.} The model's reasoning is not directly inspectable, and
the conditions of its training are only partially known. For this
reason the assistant is treated as a fallible drafting collaborator
whose outputs require verification, not as an independent source of
evidence.

This section is intended to satisfy the responsible-use expectations
that are emerging across the qualitative-research community --- including
the editorial guidance of the Academy of Management and similar bodies
--- which call for transparent disclosure of GenAI use, a clear statement
of researcher responsibility for the final text, and an account of how
risks to integrity and interpretation were managed.
