# Institutional Theory — Core Framework
## Lens for EIC Pathfinder & STEP Scale Up Analysis

---

## Scott's Three-Pillar Model (1995/2014)

**Central question:** Why do organisations behave the way they do?

Institutions constrain and shape behaviour through three distinct mechanisms:

### Regulative Pillar
Organisations comply because they **have to**. Rules, laws, sanctions. Deviation results in exclusion or penalty. Legitimacy is conferred through compliance.

> *In this analysis:* TRL eligibility thresholds, the three-strike exclusion rule, the IP retention clause, the STEP Regulation as legal mandate. The EIC compels behaviour through formal authority.

### Normative Pillar
Organisations comply because they **ought to**. Values, role expectations, professional standards. No one forces you, but deviation marks you as illegitimate — not the right kind of actor. Legitimacy is conferred through appropriateness.

> *In this analysis:* The expectation that applicants are "visionary scientist-entrepreneurs," the obligation to serve European strategic interests, the gender balance requirement. Applicants conform to be seen as appropriate.

### Cognitive Pillar
Organisations comply because they **cannot imagine doing otherwise**. Shared schemas and taken-for-granted assumptions make certain things seem natural and others unthinkable. Legitimacy is conferred through conformity with what is self-evident.

> *In this analysis:* The assumption that innovation is linear (TRL 1→4→8→market), that "deep tech" is a self-evident category, that Europe is obviously falling behind the US. No one argues for these — they are the invisible premise.

### Why It Matters
The three pillars show that the EIC instruments do not merely regulate behaviour through rules — they also shape what applicants *want to be* (normative) and what they *think is possible* (cognitive). This reveals how institutional power operates across multiple registers simultaneously.

## DiMaggio & Powell's Isomorphic Mechanisms (1983)

**Central question:** Why do organisations in the same field end up looking similar to each other?

*Isomorphism* = convergence through pressure. Three sources:

### Coercive Isomorphism
Organisations conform because a powerful actor **forces** them to. Laws, mandates, conditions attached to funding.

> *In this analysis:* The STEP Regulation legally mandates the programme's technology scope. KYC/AML compliance is non-negotiable. Applicants conform or are excluded.

### Mimetic Isomorphism
Organisations conform because they **copy** what appears to work, especially under uncertainty. When the right approach is unclear, actors imitate successful or prestigious models.

> *In this analysis:* The EIC copies VC industry structures (pitch decks, expert juries, equity instruments) and benchmarks explicitly against DARPA and Asian innovation systems. It imitates because those models carry prestige and perceived effectiveness.

### Normative Isomorphism
Organisations conform because **professional communities** diffuse shared standards and practices through training, credentialling, and networks.

> *In this analysis:* The FTO analysis requirement spreads IP profession norms. The business plan format spreads VC professional norms. Applicants who do not speak these languages are filtered out.

### Why It Matters
Isomorphism explains how the EIC shapes the broader European innovation field — not just the companies it funds, but all applicants who conform to its templates, and other funders who recognise the Sovereignty Seal. The EIC is not only a funder; it is an isomorphic pressure on the field.

## How the Two Frameworks Relate

They answer complementary questions and operate at different levels:

| | Scott's Pillars | DiMaggio & Powell |
|---|---|---|
| **Question** | How does an institution exert influence? | Why does an organisational field converge? |
| **Level** | The instrument itself (the Work Programme) | The field the instrument operates in |
| **Focus** | Mechanisms within a single instrument | Pressures spreading across many organisations |

The frameworks also map onto each other:
- Coercive isomorphism → produces regulative-pillar compliance
- Normative isomorphism → produces normative-pillar conformity
- Mimetic isomorphism → reflects cognitive-pillar schemas imported from other fields

### Analytical Payoff
The combination allows an argument such as: *EIC Pathfinder exerts primarily normative and cognitive influence on the research field, while STEP exerts primarily coercive and mimetic influence on the commercial scale-up field.* That difference is substantively meaningful — it reveals how the EU uses the two instruments for different kinds of institutional work.
