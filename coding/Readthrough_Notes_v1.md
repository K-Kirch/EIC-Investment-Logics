## Organizing Principle

### Objective

This document records a **deductive first-pass readthrough** of the EIC Work Programme 2026 (Pathfinder & STEP Scale Up), applying an institutional logics coding framework (Friedland & Alford 1991; Thornton et al. 2012) to identify which institutional logics — Science, Market, State, Profession — govern each passage.

This is a **conservative** first pass. The purpose is to code what the text clearly signals, not what can be inferred. Coding decisions made here will be revisited and refined in a subsequent inductive update pass, where patterns identified during the deductive pass (see Emerging Analytical Patterns section) will be examined systematically.

The document serves two simultaneous functions: a **working notes file** during the readthrough, and a **historical record** of the analytical decisions made. Both must be preserved.

---

### Rules

#### Document Management

1. **Never delete.** Entries are permanent. If an entry is superseded, revised, or corrected, add an addendum — do not remove or overwrite.
2. **Entries are numbered sequentially** in the order they were raised during the readthrough, not in document order. This preserves the authentic analytical history.
3. **Every entry follows the standard structure** (see template below). Earlier entries pre-date the template and are preserved as written; the template applies from [011] onward.
4. **Unresolved questions are flagged `[?]`** within their entry and listed in the Unresolved Questions Index.
5. **Cross-references** between related entries are noted explicitly (e.g., _"See also [005]"_).
6. **One passage = One entry.** Passages are divided at passage breaks in the source document. A **passage** is a single paragraph as delineated by a paragraph break — a complete, self-contained unit of text. The break is the primary boundary signal; where the extracted source text renders formatting inconsistently, completeness of meaning takes precedence. Bullet lists with an introductory sentence are treated as a single passage. Standalone headings or single-sentence fragments that cannot be coded independently may be combined with an adjacent passage, noted in the entry.

#### Analytical Standards

7. **Logic must organise the passage, not merely appear.** A logic is coded when it governs the passage — i.e., when it determines what counts as valuable, who holds authority, or what the evaluative frame is. The presence of logic vocabulary, actor types, or subject matter does not in itself constitute a coding. Ask: _is this logic shaping what the passage does, or is it the topic being discussed?_

8. **Primary coding threshold.** Each entry records only logics that clearly cross the threshold of organising the passage. Logics present at vocabulary level, as undertones, or embedded in otherwise procedural text are noted as observations but not coded. The standard is conservative: when in doubt, do not code.

9. **Purely procedural passages receive no coding.** Administrative mechanics, deadline notices, eligibility lists without argued rationale, and process descriptions are coded as "None — purely procedural." Faint signals may be noted as observations.

10. **LOGIC_TENSION** is coded only when conflict between logics is **surface-visible in the text itself** — stated, juxtaposed, or structurally legible from the passage alone. Tension that must be argued into the text does not qualify. Passages where logics are presented as complementary without acknowledging conflict represent the **tension-dissolution pattern** (Pattern A) — a related but distinct phenomenon.

11. **Science logic** requires that scientific governance is operative: peer review invoked, researcher-led agenda, scientific merit as the decisive criterion. Science as subject matter or descriptive vocabulary does not constitute LOGIC_SCIENCE.

12. **TRL** — as a professional gatekeeping instrument determining eligibility = LOGIC_PROFESSION; as an operational metric describing development stage = below threshold; as a scope-definitional parenthetical = below threshold.

13. **State logic in purely administrative capacity** — the state acting as funder (allocating budget, administering grants) does not rise to LOGIC_STATE. The state is coded when it is acting as regulator, ecosystem architect, social policy enforcer, or geopolitical actor with an argued rationale.

14. **Eligibility criteria** — procedural when they define administrative categories without rationale. LOGIC_STATE when the rationale is explicitly geopolitical or security-driven and stated in the text.

15. **Vocabulary does not determine logic.** Market vocabulary in a science-governed passage, or professional vocabulary in an administrative passage, does not automatically trigger those codes. The organising framework — what the passage is _for_ and what it treats as authoritative — determines the coding.

## Emerging Analytical Patterns

_These patterns were identified inductively during the deductive readthrough. They are documented here for systematic treatment in the inductive update pass. No entries have been modified; this section supplements the existing record._

---

### Pattern A — Tension-Dissolution

**Description:** Logics are presented as naturally complementary, and the text explicitly dissolves the tension between them by treating them as equivalent or mutually reinforcing — without acknowledging that they can and do conflict in practice.

**Analytical significance:** Tension-dissolution is itself a legitimacy move. When an institution presents two potentially competing logics as seamlessly aligned, it performs institutional coherence rather than arguing for it. The absence of acknowledged conflict is not neutrality — it is a rhetorical act. These passages are likely to carry significant analytical weight in the inductive pass as evidence of how the EIC manages institutional contradictions.

**Distinguishing feature from LOGIC_TENSION:** LOGIC_TENSION requires the conflict to be surface-visible in the text. Tension-dissolution passages present the logics as complementary — the tension is structurally present but rhetorically suppressed. These are related but distinct phenomena.

**Instances identified:**

- **[005]** — State mandate (widening countries) translated into professional mechanism (expert selection) without specifying how the two criteria are weighted when they conflict.
- **[006] / [007]** — All four logics compressed into a single sentence as naturally complementary: "presented as naturally complementary. This is a legitimacy claim in itself."
- **[010]** — EIC Board advice and EU policy priorities co-listed as equivalent inputs without acknowledging that professional and political sources of authority can conflict.
- **[019]** — "Deal flow of top-level European innovators" (market logic) and "develop the innovation ecosystem in Europe" (state logic) presented as the same activity while resting on different assumptions about why the network matters.
- **[039]** — State purpose (European Innovation Ecosystem) and professional mechanism (KICs, BAS, talent pipelines) presented as complementary, consistent with the pattern in [005] and [010].
- **[057]** — Market success (new markets, disruptive innovations) and state/collective success (economy, environment, society, global challenges) presented as equivalent and complementary impact dimensions.
- **[061]** — Mandate for open exchange ("interact and exchange") within a portfolio that includes competing approaches — science logic behaviour mandated within a competitively structured instrument without acknowledging the tension.
- **[082]** — Excellence criterion (Pathfinder Challenges): state-defined Challenge relevance embedded as the primary sub-criterion of scientific excellence, presented as complementary to novelty and methodology without acknowledging that they can conflict. Particularly strong instance — the dissolution is structurally consequential, shaping how applicants must frame their science to be funded.

### Pattern B — Coercive Governance Cluster

**Description:** A cluster of passages ([024]–[027], [061]) in which EIC Programme Managers hold discretionary authority over capital continuity, roadmap direction, and portfolio placement. The authority is stated in legally neutral language (grant agreement mechanics, milestone reviews) but the operative power structure is significant: a hired technocrat, not a peer scientist or elected official, controls whether funding continues.

**Analytical significance:** This pattern is in structural tension with the autonomy protections stated in [021] ("Awardees will bear no obligation"). The EIC formally protects researcher autonomy while retaining hard override authority over capital continuity. Across the cluster, authority escalates progressively:

- [021] — Autonomy stated: "Awardees will bear no obligation."
- [023] — Milestone reviews "overseen by EIC Programme Managers."
- [024] — Suspension/termination for failure to meet milestones; termination for "lost economic or technological relevance."
- [025] — Projects "may be requested to join" thematic portfolios — formally optional but state-directed.
- [026] — Programme Manager "establishes a common roadmap and proactively steers the portfolio"; Agency may suspend/terminate if no amendment can be agreed.
- [027] — "Relevant Commission services" named as direct participant in portfolio activities.
- [061] — Programme Manager with directive authority over Challenge portfolio; dual responsiveness requirement embedded without resolution mechanism.

**Key inductive question:** Whether this coercive governance layer constitutes a distinct institutional logic (administrative-managerial) or represents `LOGIC_STATE` operating through professional instruments — and whether the gap between the stated autonomy principle and operative control structure is a structural contradiction or a deliberate institutional design choice.

### Pattern C — State Social Equity Template

**Description:** Near-identical passages embedding social equity mandates (gender balance, female researcher empowerment, underrepresented groups) appear across multiple instruments and sections with minimal variation. The recurrence suggests a standardised state logic insert applied systematically rather than passages tailored to their immediate context.

**Analytical significance:** The template character of these passages is analytically significant in two ways. First, the repetition suggests the mandates originate at EU legislative level (Horizon Europe Regulation, EU gender mainstreaming obligations) and are being reproduced as compliance text, not articulated as a programmatic commitment unique to the EIC. Second, the same text appearing in both Pathfinder Open and Pathfinder Challenges sections ([047] and [063]) suggests the template is deployed regardless of instrument design context.

**Instances identified:**

- **[001]** — Strategic Goal 1: "in particular from underrepresented groups such as women innovators and those from less developed ecosystems."
- **[017]** — Gender and Diversity Innovation Index as institutional accountability instrument for state-mandated priorities.
- **[047]** — EIC Pathfinder Open team composition: "You are particularly encouraged to empower female researchers in your project and to achieve gender balance among your work package leaders."
- **[063]** — EIC Pathfinder Challenges team composition: near-identical text to [047]; note in entry confirms recurrence as a standard template.

**Connection to [059]:** The tiebreaker paragraph ranks gender balance above SME participation and geographic representation as a funding decision criterion — confirming that social equity is a structurally embedded state priority, not merely rhetorical text.

### [001] — Underrepresented groups in Strategic Goal 1

**Document location:** I. Introduction — Strategic Goals and KPIs / Strategic Goal 1
**Programme:** [SHARED]

**Source text:**

> _"To be the investor of choice for those with visionary ideas: The EIC must have continent-wide recognition and traction with high potential start-ups, entrepreneurs and innovative researchers, in particular from underrepresented groups such as women innovators and those from less developed ecosystems."_

**Question:** Which logic governs the focus on underrepresented groups?

**Analysis:** Code as `LOGIC_STATE`. The state does not only pursue geopolitical and industrial interests — it also pursues social policy goals: equality, inclusion, and equitable access to public resources. When an EU funding instrument directs attention toward underrepresented groups, it is the state using its funding lever to achieve a social outcome that the market would not produce and that no professional body has authority to mandate. The obligation is political — EU gender mainstreaming and non-discrimination principles are embedded in the Horizon Europe Regulation as legal commitments.

This is not `LOGIC_PROFESSION` — professional DEI norms have spread widely, but professional logic governs evaluation authority, not conditions imposed on applicants by a state-funded programme. It does not require a new logic. It is the same state logic operating on a social equity dimension rather than a geopolitical one. Both are expressions of the state pursuing collective interests that markets do not serve.

The passage warrants `LOGIC_TENSION` alongside `LOGIC_STATE`. A strategic goal that opens with market performance indicators and professional evaluation criteria, then closes with a social inclusion requirement, shows the state inserting a social policy obligation into an economically-oriented instrument. LOGIC_STATE is doing double work here — justifying intervention on both geopolitical and social equity grounds simultaneously.

**Coding:** `LOGIC_STATE` + `LOGIC_TENSION` (paired with whichever market or profession codes apply to the earlier part of the passage)

**Addendum — analytical standard note:** `LOGIC_TENSION` was coded based on a constructed argument about the state performing dual work. Under the conservative deductive standard established from [011] onward, tension must be surface-visible in the text itself. Whether this tension meets that threshold should be reviewed in the inductive update.

### [002] — "Crowd-in investments" in Strategic Goal 2

**Document location:** I. Introduction — Strategic Goals and KPIs / Strategic Goal 2
**Programme:** [SHARED]

**Source text:**

> _"To crowd in EUR 30-50 billion investment into European deep tech: The EIC must bridge a critical financing gap faced by deep tech companies and leverage the EIC Fund to influence the allocation of private assets in their support."_

**Question:** Which logic does this represent, and is there a logic tension?

**Analysis:** Code as `LOGIC_MARKET` + `LOGIC_STATE`. Crowd-in investments primarily represents `LOGIC_MARKET` — the objective treats private investor participation as both the mechanism and the measure of success. The programme succeeds when private capital follows public capital. Market confidence is the ultimate validator of quality. The vocabulary ("investments", "crowd-in") is drawn entirely from financial market logic.

`LOGIC_STATE` is also present. The state crowds in investment precisely because the market is not doing it on its own — this is the state acting as market corrector. The public funding exists to de-risk the investment sufficiently to attract private actors who would otherwise stay out. The rationale is state logic; the mechanism and goal are market logic.

The state is setting an objective that measures its own success by market criteria — meaning the state subordinates its authority to market validation. This is the `MARKET ↔ STATE` tension running deeper than instrument design: the state is not just using a market instrument to achieve a state goal, it is defining success in market terms.

Additional observations: "bridge a critical financing gap" asserts market failure as a premise rather than arguing for it — the state logic rests on a cognitive assumption (`COG_MARKETFAILURE`). "leverage the EIC Fund to influence the allocation of private assets" is the state explicitly redirecting private capital decisions — state logic asserting itself over market logic while using market vocabulary; the tension is embedded in the wording itself.

**Coding:** `LOGIC_MARKET` + `LOGIC_STATE` + `LOGIC_TENSION`

**Addendum — analytical standard note:** `COG_MARKETFAILURE` was introduced as a cognitive assumption tag not part of the established four-logic framework. `LOGIC_TENSION` is argued rather than surface-visible. Both should be reviewed against the conservative standard in the inductive update.

### [003] — Strategic Goal 5: Science, market, and state logics

**Document location:** I. Introduction — Strategic Goals and KPIs / Strategic Goal 5
**Programme:** [SHARED]

**Source text:**

> _"To catalyse innovation impacts from European public research and innovation: The EIC must build partnerships to draw on, and commercialise, the best ideas from the research base across the EU, and scale-up start-ups funded under other EU or national initiatives."_

**Question:** What is the full logic analysis of this goal?

**Analysis:** All three logics are present but performing different functions.

`LOGIC_SCIENCE` is the _origin point_ — "the research base across the EU" and "European public research" position science as the upstream source of value. However, science is the raw material here, not the measure of success. Science logic is present but not organising.

`LOGIC_MARKET` is the _conversion mechanism_ — "commercialise the best ideas" and "scale-up start-ups" are market logic. The goal measures success by market conversion of scientific outputs. "Impacts" means scalable, market-facing results, not societal impact in a normative sense. "The best ideas" are implicitly those with commercial potential, not greatest scientific merit — a quiet reframing of what counts as valuable science.

`LOGIC_STATE` is the _structural frame_ — "build partnerships", "EU or national initiatives", and "European public research" signal the state as architect. The EIC actively constructs pipelines between public research and commercial actors. Positioning the EIC as coordinator of the broader European public funding ecosystem is a state logic move.

Two logic tensions are present: (1) `SCIENCE ↔ MARKET` — science is subordinated to market logic, the research base valuable only insofar as it produces commercialisable ideas; (2) `MARKET ↔ STATE` — the state constructs the partnerships and pipelines that make commercial conversion happen, driving market activity while market vocabulary provides the measure of success.

**Coding:** `LOGIC_SCIENCE` + `LOGIC_MARKET` + `LOGIC_STATE` + `LOGIC_TENSION`

**Addendum — possible over-coding of `LOGIC_SCIENCE`:** The codebook defines LOGIC*SCIENCE by what \_organises* a passage: peer review, researcher-led agenda, scientific merit as the decisive criterion. None of those are present in this text. "The research base across the EU" is the subject matter being referenced, not a signal that science logic is shaping the evaluative framework. Coding it risks conflating science as a _topic_ with science as an institutional _logic_. To be reviewed during inductive update.

### [004] — Strategic Goal 6: Professional logic evaluated through market success criteria

**Document location:** I. Introduction — Strategic Goals and KPIs / Strategic Goal 6
**Programme:** [SHARED]

**Source text:**

> _"To achieve operational excellence: The agility and speed of EIC operations and decision making must align with the expectations of applicants, investors and market norms."_

**Question:** Is the reading of LOGIC_PROFESSION evaluated through market criteria correct?

**Analysis:** Yes — and the tension is analytically significant. `LOGIC_PROFESSION` is the primary logic. "Operational excellence" is a professional management standard — the EIC measures itself against the performance norms of a well-run organisation, where process quality, responsiveness, and decision-making efficiency are the criteria of legitimacy. "Agility and speed" are professional competencies, not scientific or political virtues.

The market dimension is precise: the benchmark for professional excellence is not set internally or by peer public institutions — it is set by "investors and market norms". The EIC is not aspiring to operate like a rigorous scientific body or a well-run public agency. It is aspiring to operate like a well-run investor.

`PROFESSION ↔ MARKET` tension: the EIC is a public institution whose professional standards are defined by private market expectations. The state's formal governance obligations (accountability, transparency, due process) constrain agility in ways private investors are not bound by; the goal quietly papers over that constraint by aspirationally aligning with market norms, leaving the tension implicit rather than resolved.

**Coding:** `LOGIC_PROFESSION` + `LOGIC_MARKET` + `LOGIC_TENSION`

**Addendum — analytical standard note:** `LOGIC_TENSION` rests on an argument that the passage "quietly papers over" a constraint — an interpretive move rather than a surface-visible tension. Review against conservative standard in the inductive update.

### [005] — Concluding paragraph of the Strategic Goals section: State mandate and professional mechanism

**Document location:** I. Introduction — Strategic Goals and KPIs / concluding paragraph
**Programme:** [SHARED]

**Source text:**

> _"In addition, the EIC Board has published a set of recommendations to improve the participation of innovators from widening countries in the EIC. **Question:** Is there a STATE ↔ PROFESSION tension here?

**Addendum — analytical standard note:** The tension here is expressed as absence of acknowledgment rather than explicit conflict — a tension-dissolution reading. ### [176] — VI. STEP Scale Up: Table 9 — Level of risk: Risk level of the investment

**Document location:** VI. EIC STEP Scale Up / How does the EIC decide? / Table 9 Award Criteria — Level of risk, implementation, and need for Union support (Risk level cell)
**Programme:** [STEP]

**Source text:**

> _"Risk level of the investment: Does the nature and level of risk of the investment in your innovation mean that European market actors are unwilling to commit the full amount that is needed without an investment from the EIC Fund?"_

**Question:** What logics are present?

**Analysis:** The market failure rationale from [130] and [146] is operationalised here as an explicit evaluation criterion: the company must demonstrate that private capital is insufficient without EIC co-investment. `LOGIC_STATE` organises — market failure as the state's evaluative criterion for its own intervention.

**Observation — structural mandate for circular market validation:** This criterion, read alongside the pre-commitment requirement in [141], creates a structural mandate for the circularity identified in [140]. To satisfy both simultaneously — demonstrated market interest AND demonstrated market failure — the only coherent path is to use EIC's prospective participation as the lever to obtain the private pre-commitment. Sophisticated founders approach private investors with the EIC application as the anchor: "We are applying for EUR 10–30M from EIC; commit now and your investment will be 3–5x leveraged." The private investor's commitment is conditional on EIC participation. The EIC then uses that conditional commitment as proof of market interest. Independent market validation is structurally eliminated: the "market interest" criterion tests whether the market believes in the company _with_ EIC co-investment, not independently of it — a materially lower bar.

Crucially, EIC holds a 20–33% anchor position in the round (EUR 10–30M of a EUR 50–150M total). At this scale, private investors' decisions are materially conditioned on EIC participation, not merely signalled by it. The instrument has not designed around the circularity — it has mandated it.

**See also:** [130], [140], [141], [145].

**Coding:** `LOGIC_STATE`

**[?] Flag:** The co-presence of market interest (pre-commitment, [141]) and market failure (this criterion) as simultaneous eligibility and award requirements structurally mandates circular market validation: EIC's prospective participation constructs the market signal the state then requires as proof of market legitimacy. With EIC holding a 20–33% anchor position, private co-investors are materially dependent on EIC participation — independent market validation is by design unavailable. Whether this represents a fundamental contradiction in the instrument's logic (claiming to test market readiness while eliminating independent market assessment) or a deliberate developmental state design choice (the state constructing the market conditions it requires) is the central theoretical question for the STEP analysis; consolidates and sharpens [132], [140], [145]; revisit in inductive pass.
